Foundation stage

Junior Block

This stage is the first step into structured school life. We keep the pace calm and supportive so children can settle in, communicate better, and build comfort with routines.

Communication Play-based learning Daily routines
Back to programs Next stage

What this stage supports

GoalConfidence in the classroom
MethodVisual cues, repetition, and guidance
OutcomeBetter attention and participation

What we focus on

Children in the Junior Block are introduced to school routines in a gentle way. We work on communication, social comfort, motor coordination, and simple classroom participation.

Learning areas

  • Language readiness and basic expression
  • Fine-motor and gross-motor practice
  • Matching, sorting, and simple early concepts
  • Play activities that build attention and memory

How we teach

Sessions are short, guided, and highly interactive. Teachers use songs, repetition, objects, pictures, and simple prompts so each child can respond at a comfortable pace.

Typical day

Arrival settling in and greeting time
Warm-up songs, movement, and focus exercises
Learning block guided tasks and practice
Activity time play, art, or sensory support

Skills we nurture

  • Settling into a classroom
  • Following one-step instructions
  • Using words, gestures, or cues
  • Staying engaged for longer periods
